About Turkistan

The province of Turkistan reflects the rich history of Kazakhstan, historically important cities on the Great Silk Road are located here,
as well as the variety of national dishes and hospitality that amazes every guest who visits there. Turkistan is a culturally and historically important city
of Kazakhstan. The ancient city was named the spiritual capital of the Turkic world at an informal summit of Turkic-speaking states and was officially
included in the list of world heritage cities in Eurasia in 2021. It is worth exploring the architecture of the sacred buildings built.
Complex engineering solutions, in addition to the old ones, there are also new districts, which are the beginning of a new head of the city.

Attractions In Turkestan

Shymkent City

Shymkent is one of the oldest cities in Kazakhstan with a developed infrastructure, with high tourist potential, located at the crossroads of the Great Silk Road. The city attracts with its warm sunny weather, delicious barbecue, and pilaf, southern hospitality.

Turkistan Region, Shymkent

Khoja Ahmed Yasawi Mausoleum

The mausoleum of Khoja Ahmed Yasavi is a very sacred place, which is an outstanding historical landmark of the Turkestan region and all of Kazakhstan. This grandiose structure was erected in honor of Ahmet Yassaui, a famous ancient Turkic poet and preacher of Sufism, who lived in the 12th century. When visiting this place, it is recommended to spend the night here in order to gain good spiritual energy. The mausoleum of Khoja Akhmet Yassaui is included in the UNESCO World Heritage List.

Akmeshit Cave

The Akmeshit cave beckons with its mystery and attracts scientists and tourists from all over the republic. Nobody knows how this cave appeared because for millions of years it was closed, that is,it had no connection with the outside world, but in the 20th century, part of it collapsed and mankind got a chance to get inside.

How to get there Getting Around
  • By Plane

    Air Astana is the national air carrier of Kazakhstan, based in Almaty.
    It operates regular international and domestic flights on 64 routes from its main hub, Almaty International Airport,
    and from a secondary hub, Nursultan Nazarbayev International Airport.

  • By Train

    If you are already in another city of Kazakhstan, you can get to Turkistan Province by train. You should choose Shymkent or Turkistan as the destination city.

  • By Bus
